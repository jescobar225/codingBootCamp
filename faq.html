FAQ

We know that this is a big decision and one that will impact the life of you, anyone that lives in your household, and the pet you will adopt. We know you are probably full of questions and in order to ease a lot of those concerns we have compiled some of the most frequently asked questions regarding pet adoptions across the internet.

What are some reasons to adopt a pet instead of buying one?

- When adopting a pet you are saving a life, combating the practice of "puppy mills," getting a great animal, and helping more than just one animal. It has also been proven that having a pet is physically, emotionally, and psychologically beneficial. Animals give you unconditional love, give you a sense of purpose and fulfillment, and lessen feelings of loneliness. These are just a few of the many benefits of adopting instead of buying a pet.

What is a "puppy mill?"

- Puppy mills are operations in which animals are bred factory-style, usually in very poor conditions for both the parents and the puppies. These living conditions result in poor health and behavioral issues for the animals being bred.

Why are adopted pets usually such great animals?

- Pets adopted from a shelter usually make great pets because many of them are there due to human problems like a break-up or a move where the pet wasn't taken into consideration. These animals are often happy and healthy and ready for someone to take them home. Many of these are already house-trained and love being around families.

Is it expensive to adopt from a shelter/rescue?

- It is much cheaper to adopt a pet than to buy one from a pet store or a breeder. Most shelters/rescues have a flat adoption fee which includes neutering/spaying, vaccinations, and sometimes microchipping. Depending on what kind of animal you adopt, you could also save money on training and housebreaking expenses.
